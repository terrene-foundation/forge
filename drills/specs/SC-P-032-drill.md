## How to drill it

The practitioner writes a 200-word paragraph. Then:

1. Identify 3 non-obvious writing choices in the paragraph (word selection, sentence structure, absence of expected elements).
2. For each choice, write a margin note: what was chosen, what alternatives were considered (at least 2), and why this choice serves the argument.
3. Exchange margin notes with a peer. Can the peer reconstruct the author's reasoning from the margin notes alone?

Scoring: number of non-obvious choices identified (are there really only 3, or did the practitioner miss some?), quality of alternatives (are they genuine alternatives, or straw men?), and reconstructibility (can a peer understand the reasoning?).
