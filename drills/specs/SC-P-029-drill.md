## How to drill it

The practitioner selects a section of their draft containing 3 specific claims. For each claim:

1. Define 3-4 search categories relevant to the claim (narrower than the initial search).
2. Search for papers published in the last 3 years that address the claim.
3. Classify each paper found as MUST CITE, SHOULD CITE, MAY CITE, or DO NOT CITE.
4. For DO NOT CITE papers, explain the positioning risk: what would citing this paper signal about your contribution?
5. Update the tier-ranked literature base from SC-P-025 with the new findings.

Scoring: number of genuine gaps found, quality of DO NOT CITE reasoning (is the positioning risk real?), and whether the gap check changed the practitioner's confidence in any claim.
