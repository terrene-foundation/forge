# Dual-Track Consistency

Foundation modules (F1-F4) are shared between AI Engineer and AI Business Consultant tracks. Specialization modules diverge.

## MUST

- Foundation content must be accessible to both technical and non-technical learners
- Every foundation exercise must have value for both tracks
- E-track modules may assume F1-F4 competence and technical depth
- C-track modules may assume F1-F4 competence and strategic depth
- Cross-references between tracks are encouraged (engineers understand business context, consultants understand technical constraints)

## MUST NOT

- Foundation modules must not require deep coding skills (F3 is hands-on but guided)
- C-track modules must not require writing production code
- E-track modules must not skip business context ("why does this matter?")
- Track-specific content must not duplicate foundation content

**Why:** The shared foundation ensures a common vocabulary. Divergence after F4 allows depth without forcing inappropriate skills on either track.
